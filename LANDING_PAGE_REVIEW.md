# Landing Page Review

## Ranking

1. Variant 3 - Investor / Category Creation
2. Variant 2 - Club Operator / Pain Killer
3. Variant 6 - Ultra-Minimal / Apple-Like
4. Variant 5 - Pickleball / Wedge
5. Variant 1 - Manifesto / Worldview
6. Variant 4 - Product / How It Works

## Strongest Hero

Variant 3: "The conversation layer for real-world community building."

Reason: it names the category, implies scale, and does not collapse the company into pickleball or a staff tool.

## Strongest Tagline

"AI for the conversations that build community."

Reason: it is short, human, and specific enough to separate Human Conversation from chatbot, CRM, and generic community software positioning.

## Strongest Visual Direction

Variant 3.

The abstract relationship graph feels like useful relationship memory without overexplaining the system. It is the most investor-ready visual system.

## Top Risks

- The word "autonomous" can overstate launch behavior unless paired with human confirmation.
- The operator page may sell faster than the investor page, but it makes the company feel smaller if used alone.
- The pickleball wedge is sharp, but the public home page should not trap the company in one sport.
- Proof copy must stay disciplined. The NEPC acceptance numbers are framed as early NEPC proof, not universal claims.
- The fake form needs a real intake path before public launch.

## What To Test Next

Run Variant 3 with investors and Variant 2 with club operators. The decision metric is which page earns a concrete next conversation fastest.

## Recommendation

Use Variant 3 as the default HumanConversation.com direction. Keep Variant 2 as the sales page for club operators and Variant 5 as the vertical wedge page.
